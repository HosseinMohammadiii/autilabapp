This large-scale cross-sectional study of children aged 5 to 12 years attending schools across all Cyprus districts found a 1.8% prevalence of ASD, with a substantial male predominance (80.3%). ASD prevalence was significantly lower in primary schools and markedly higher in special schools compared to pre-primary schools. Most children with ASD received support primarily through speech-language therapy and special education. Common comorbidities included attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (37.6%) and intellectual disability (10.7%)
