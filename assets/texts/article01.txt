Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a complex, lifelong neurodevelopmental condition marked by difficulties in social communication and repetitive behaviors. Over recent decades, its prevalence has increased significantly, prompting ongoing research into genetic and environmental factors that may contribute to its development. While many mechanisms such as oxidative stress, mitochondrial dysfunction, immune dysregulation, and neuroinflammation have been proposed, the exact causes remain largely unclear.
ASD presents with wide clinical variability, often accompanied by other medical or intellectual conditions. Early diagnosis and intervention, particularly through advanced cognitive-behavioral educational techniques, are crucial for improving outcomes. Pediatricians play a vital role in identifying early signs and facilitating timely specialist evaluation. Though no cure exists, treatments focus on managing symptoms and comorbidities, with intellectual functioning during childhood being a key factor influencing long-term prognosis.
This comprehensive update highlights the evolving understanding of ASD’s biology, clinical features, and treatment approaches, emphasizing the importance of continued research and early support.
