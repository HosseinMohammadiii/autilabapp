Researchers from Karolinska Institutet in Sweden have developed an AI-based screening system to help identify toddlers at elevated risk of autism. The system uses machine learning to analyze data from over 30,000 children, focusing on measures like age at first smile and eating habits. Initial tests showed the model effectively identified ASD in various age groups, with peak accuracy at 84.2% in children aged two to four. Experts caution that the algorithm is not a substitute for clinical methods and highlight the risk of misidentifying non-autistic children.
