This study examines whether children diagnosed with ASD between 12 to 36 months continue to meet diagnostic criteria at 5 to 7 years of age. Findings suggest that a substantial percentage of individuals clinically diagnosed with ASD in early childhood no longer met criteria for ASD at school age. Higher baseline adaptive scores and female sex were associated with nonpersistent ASD. The study emphasizes the need for ongoing developmental monitoring and diagnostic clarification over time.
