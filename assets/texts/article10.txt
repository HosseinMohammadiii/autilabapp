Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) continues to show rising prevalence among children, with recent surveillance across 16 U.S. sites revealing that approximately 1 in 31 children aged 8 years were identified with ASD in 2022. Significant disparities were observed by sex, race, and geography, with boys and children from Asian or Pacific Islander, Black, American Indian or Alaska Native, and Hispanic backgrounds showing higher rates than White children. Notably, the incidence of co-occurring intellectual disability was also greater among these historically underrepresented groups. Early identification has improved over time, as evidenced by higher diagnostic rates among children born in 2018 compared to those born in 2014. Despite this progress, substantial variability remains across regions in diagnostic practices and access to autism-specific testing, highlighting the need for more equitable and standardized early evaluation strategies to ensure timely support and intervention for all children with ASD.
