Verbal communication challenges in adolescence are a significant concern among individuals with autism spectrum disorder (ASD), yet limited attention has been given to how these difficulties develop beyond early childhood and how they compare to those experienced by individuals with other developmental disabilities (DD). Understanding the factors that contribute to persistent communication issues later in life is essential for shaping effective support strategies. Differences in early expressive language abilities, emotional well-being, and family background—including maternal education level and race—may all influence long-term verbal outcomes. By examining these variables, new insights can be gained into the developmental trajectories of communication skills and the social contexts that shape them across diverse populations.
